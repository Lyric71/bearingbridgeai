# PAGE 2 — METHOD (/azimuth)

### Hero
**H1:** AZIMUTH
**Sub:** An azimuth is the exact bearing a navigator steers by. Not a vision. Not a moonshot. A precise, checkable direction — recalculated as conditions change.

### Intro paragraph
AI is moving too fast for five-year transformation plans and too consequentially for improvisation. AZIMUTH is our answer: a disciplined path from honest assessment to operating reality, with proof gates between every promise and every investment.

### The five phases (each gets a section: name, question it answers, what we do, what you get)

**01 · Bearings** — *Where are you, really?*
Interviews across functions, systems and data audit, current AI usage and skills mapping. No flattery: the deliverable is an honest state-of-AI assessment, including what's working that you should leave alone.

**02 · Chart** — *Where is value — and where isn't it?*
Use-case portfolio ranked by impact × feasibility. Model strategy across both ecosystems. Governance baseline. Explicit no-go zones: the use cases we recommend you *don't* pursue, and why.

**03 · Crossing** — *Can it survive contact with your operations?*
One or two use cases, piloted against success and kill criteria agreed before we start. Pilots that don't earn their way to production get killed — in writing, with the lessons documented.

**04 · Build** — *Ship what survived.*
Production deployment: integrations, security, the winning model for each job — Chinese or Western. Built to be operated by your team, not to make you dependent on ours.

**05 · Anchor** — *Does it stick when we leave?*
Training, governance embedded in workflows rather than documents, adoption measurement, handover. The phase most consultancies skip, because it ends the engagement. We think it's the point.

**Horizon (continuous)** — *What's changing on both sides of the world?*
Model watch and regulation watch (EU AI Act, China's AI measures, Middle East frameworks), quarterly roadmap refresh. AI shifts monthly; your bearing should too.

### Closing block
**H2:** Why the kill criteria matter.
Anyone can show you a demo. The question is what happens when the demo meets your data, your processes, and your people. We put failure conditions in the contract because that's what taking your money seriously looks like.
**CTA:** Book a call

## Design & Style Recommendation (implementation spec for Claude Code)

Reference: https://terracotta-three.vercel.app/ — "warm editorial" style. Extracted June 11, 2026 from live CSS. The look fits our positioning unusually well: it reads like a serious publication, not a SaaS landing page — calm, confident, zero hype. Keep the system, adapt the accent story to our brand.

### Stack
Next.js (App Router) + Tailwind CSS. Single accent color discipline. No component library needed; the design is intentionally spare.

### Design tokens (extracted, then adapted)

```css
:root {
  --color-cream:   #F3E9D8;  /* rgb(243,233,216) — hero & alternating section bg */
  --color-ink:     #111827;  /* headlines, dark bands, footer bg */
  --color-accent:  #C56A3C;  /* terracotta — buttons, accent words, numerals.
                                Option: shift toward a deeper cinnabar/Chinese
                                lacquer red (#A63D2F) to quietly carry the
                                China story. Pick ONE and never add a second. */
  --color-paper:   #FFFFFF;  /* content sections */
  --color-success: #16A34A;  /* checkmarks in lists only */
}
```

Text on cream/paper: ink at 100% for headings, ~70–80% opacity for body (source uses lab() with .6/.7/.8 alphas). Text on ink sections: cream at 60–80%.

### Typography (extracted)
- **Display serif** for all headings: source uses `ui-serif, Georgia` stack at weight 400 — recommend a real webfont with the same feel: **Source Serif 4** (or Lora). H1 72px/90px desktop, centered in hero; H2 48px; H3 ~20px. Never bold — the serif at regular weight is the signature.
- **Accent-italic device:** one or two words of the H1 set in italic + accent color ("Tell your story with *warmth*"). Ours: "AI adoption that survives contact with *reality*."
- **Body sans:** source falls back to Arial/Helvetica 16px/24px — recommend **Inter**, 16px/24–28px, max-width ~65ch.
- **Label style** for section kickers and phase numerals: small caps or uppercase, wide letter-spacing, accent color ("01", "THE METHOD").

### Layout patterns (extracted from page structure)
1. **Sticky white nav**, thin bottom border: wordmark left (serif), center links, one filled accent button right ("Book a call", square corners, 10px 20px padding, 14px).
2. **Cream hero band**: centered H1 (max ~16 words over two lines), 1–2 sentence sub in muted ink, two CTAs — primary filled accent (16px, 16px 32px padding, **no border-radius**), secondary white with 1px ink border. Generous vertical padding (~120–160px).
3. **White content sections** alternating with cream; one **ink-dark band** per page maximum (we'll use it for the final CTA, white/cream text).
4. **Numbered feature columns** (01 / 02 / 03 in accent serif numerals above H3 + short paragraph) — maps perfectly to AZIMUTH phases and the three commitments block.
5. **Editorial testimonial**: oversized serif blockquote on its own band — ours will carry the founder quote or client quotes later.
6. **Pricing/offer card**: single bordered card, checkmark list — adapt as the four offer cards (border 1px ink/10%, no shadow, no radius).
7. **Square corners everywhere** (border-radius 0; the only circle is avatars). No drop shadows. Whitespace does the work.

### Per-page application
- **Home:** cream hero → white "three commitments" (numbered cols) → cream "both worlds" band with the data-residency line as small print → white method teaser (5 numerals in a row) → offer cards → ink CTA band.
- **Method (/azimuth):** cream hero with compass-bearing motif; each phase = numbered editorial section alternating white/cream; Horizon as a thin full-width accent-bordered band.
- **Services:** four cards in the pricing-card style, "fees on request" where the price would be — honesty as design.
- **Expertise/About/Insights:** long-form editorial layout, 65ch column, serif H2s — the Terracotta reading experience is the template.
- **Footer:** ink background, cream text: entities (Hong Kong · Shanghai), data note, newsletter, LinkedIn.

### Art direction notes
- Imagery: minimal. Line-drawn navigation/cartography motifs (compass rose, bearing lines, meridian grids) in accent color on cream; no stock photos of robots/brains, no gradients, no glassmorphism.
- Motion: at most a slow fade-up on section entry. The brand is calm; the market screams.
- Accessibility: accent-on-cream fails contrast for body text — accent is for accents (buttons with white text, numerals, italic words ≥24px), never body copy.
- Dark-band discipline: ink sections only for the closing CTA and footer, so they retain weight.

## Copy rules (for whoever writes next)

1. No "unlock," "supercharge," "10x," "revolutionize," "game-changing." Ever.
2. Every speed or outcome claim must be one we can defend in a contract.
3. Short sentences. Plain verbs. The reader is a senior operator who smells hype at one paragraph's distance.
4. The China point appears once per page maximum — confidence, not insistence.
5. English first; ZH/FR versions after positioning is frozen.
